What’s a game jam?

A game jam is a hackathon for video games. It is a gathering of people for the purpose of planning, designing, and creating one or more games within a short span of time, usually for 48hours, and some even longer. Participants are generally made up of programmers, game designers, artists, writers, computer science students, and others in game development-relatedfields

Breif history of game jam

In March 2002,video game developersChris HeckerandSean Barrett, interested in the capability of modern hardware inrenderinga large number ofsprites, worked withDoug Church,Jonathan Blow, andCasey Muratoriin developing a specializedgame enginecapable of rendering a massive number of sprites. Hecker and Barrett invited a small group of video game developers to meet in Hecker's office inOakland, Californiafor the purpose of creating innovative video games using this newly built engine. Hecker and Barrettnamed this gathering the0th Indie Game Jam, a game design and programming event "designed to encourage experimentation and innovation in the game industry

What’s CSE GameJam ?

Esi Game is a new version of game jam developed by scientific club esi “CSE”,as all gamejams our CSE GameJam is a contest where a theme is presented in start with a deadline(approximately after 48h) before which the contestants are obliged to finish building a gamebased on the presented theme thus it is basically a hackathone for game developers.
In addition CSE GameJam will also contain an open gallery for hackathon participants andnon-participants where they will be able to present their previously realised video games,furthermore CSE will providing various workshops for beginners interested in learning moreabout video games development Finally , since there is many competent developers with no idea how to start or with ideas noseeing the light our event will surely be good opportunity for all these developers .
